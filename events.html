Indian Festivals

In Madhuram, Festivals become a family because you start seeing the same people. We celebrate all festivals be it Holi, Diwali, Ganesh Chaturthi, Eid, Christmas, etc. These are our festivals and on these days we are all Indians. We celebrate each festival by maintaining the spirit of sisterhood, along with special meals and decorations done by students themselves as a part of recreational activity. The traditional mess menu is icing on the cake amidst the festivities and celebrations.

Parties & Celebrations

To celebrate a festival for some special occasion is one thing, but to celebrate for no reason means living life out. We understand that continuous studies and being away from home can be stressful at times. So in Madhuram, we party in the most uncommon manner. When the exams are over, or when the year is over; Music, Dance, Games, Prizes take over along with delicious starters, main course, sweets. We never forget to plan a Domino’s Pizza Party once a year.

“Strive for progress, not perfection.” – Unknown

Recreational Events

Life is best enjoyed when the time is equally divided into eating, sleeping, studying and recreational activities. We organise such activities like sketching competition, tree plantation, or some group activity for those students who need a break. These activities contribute towards a better mental health which in return helps in better learning. Some activities are just conducted for fun, while others like art competitions are to bring out the best of talents, in which, the winners are awarded. Moreover, we motivate our students to keep on sharing their talents with us even after they leave Kota so we could motivate them by posting their talents online on our website or blogs.
